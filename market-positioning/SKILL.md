# Market Positioning

Run a full positioning and competitive intelligence sprint. Analyzes competitors, identifies unclaimed positioning territory, and produces a messaging hierarchy — from core positioning statement down to homepage headlines and landing page copy.

---

## Context Collection

Before starting, gather this information. If the user has already provided it, proceed without asking:

1. **What is the product?** (what it does, who it's for, what problem it solves)
2. **Who is the ICP?** (job title, company size, pain points, what they search for)
3. **Who are the main competitors?** (direct and indirect — ask the user, then supplement with research)
4. **What stage is this?** (initial positioning, repositioning, new landing page, new market entry)
5. **Any existing messaging?** (current tagline, homepage headline, or positioning statement if one exists)

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Competitive Landscape

For each competitor (3–6 is ideal):

- Identify their **primary headline** and **subheadline**
- Identify their **positioning angle** (what they claim to be best at)
- Identify their **target customer** (who their copy speaks to)
- Note their **proof points** (metrics, logos, testimonials they lead with)
- Note any **weaknesses or gaps** in their messaging (what they avoid, what they underplay)

If the user has Exa or web search available, research competitors directly. Otherwise, ask the user to describe each competitor or paste their homepage copy.

Summarize as a comparison table:

| Competitor | Headline | Positioning Angle | Target Customer | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Step 2 — Positioning Gap Analysis

Based on the competitive landscape, identify:

- **Overcrowded angles** — what every competitor is saying (avoid these or reframe them)
- **White space** — positioning territory that is unclaimed or underserved
- **ICP mismatch** — competitors targeting slightly different customers, leaving an opening
- **Tone gap** — if all competitors sound the same (enterprise, clinical, feature-heavy), note the opportunity to differentiate on voice

Produce a short paragraph: "Everyone is saying X. Nobody is saying Y. The opportunity is Z."

### Step 3 — Positioning Statement

Write a positioning statement using this structure:

> For [ICP], [product name] is the [category] that [primary benefit] — unlike [competitor or alternative], which [key differentiator].

Produce 2–3 variations with different angles (e.g., outcome-led vs. pain-led vs. category-defining).

### Step 4 — Messaging Hierarchy

Produce a full messaging hierarchy:

**Tier 1 — Core positioning** (internal, 1–2 sentences, the strategic anchor)

**Tier 2 — Homepage headline** (3–5 options, punchy, 6–10 words each)
- Lead with outcome, pain, or bold category claim
- Avoid generic verbs: "streamline", "empower", "transform", "revolutionize"
- Use specific, visual language

**Tier 3 — Subheadline** (2–3 options, 1–2 sentences, expands on the headline)
- Who it's for + what they get + why it's different

**Tier 4 — Hero section bullets or proof points** (3–5 items)
- Specific outcomes, metrics, or capabilities
- Written from the customer's perspective

**Tier 5 — CTA copy** (3 options for the primary CTA button)

### Step 5 — Landing Page Copy (if requested)

If the user wants a full landing page or specific section copy, produce:

- **Hero section**: headline, subheadline, CTA, supporting proof point
- **Problem section**: 3–4 pain points the ICP recognizes
- **Solution section**: how the product addresses each pain point
- **Social proof**: placeholder structure for testimonials or logos
- **FAQ**: 3–5 questions that address objections

---

## Output Format

Deliver in this order:
1. Competitive landscape table
2. Positioning gap paragraph
3. Positioning statement options (2–3)
4. Messaging hierarchy
5. Landing page copy (if requested)

Keep each section clearly labeled. After delivering, ask:
- "Do you want me to go deeper on any of these sections?"
- "Want me to rewrite any headline options with a different angle?"
